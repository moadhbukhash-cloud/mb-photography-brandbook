— Color

Ink. Paper.
Everything between.

MB has no chromatic accent. The palette is two anchors and a quiet ramp of nine greys — calibrated against the warmth of paper, not the cold of pure neutral.

— Anchors

The two ends.

Ink and Paper. They appear on every surface, in some ratio. The greys live between them.

— Grey ramp

— Usage

Ratios, by surface.

Every surface picks a primary, a secondary, and one accent step from the ramp. Below: the canonical recipes for the three most common contexts.

Editorial · Light
  • 70% Paper
  • 22% Ink
  • 8% Grey 55
Editorial · Dark
  • 80% Ink
  • 12% Paper
  • 8% Grey 55
Print · Letterhead
  • 60% Bone
  • 30% Ink
  • 10% Grey 85

— Capsules

Four named extracts.

Rule of one
A single capsule governs a single edit, brief, or installation. Mixing capsules within one piece is permitted only when the subject itself spans them (a coastline meeting a desert; a mountain seen through a city window).
