— Voice

Considered,
never chatty.

The way the brand writes is the way it designs — restrained, specific, and honest about what it knows. This page is the rule book for words; the type page is the rule book for the letters they're set in.

— Pillars

Four pairs.

Each principle carries its shadow — the failure mode it walks past. Hold both in view to write well.

— 01

Confident,
not arrogant.

Speak with authority about the craft. Let the work do the talking. Avoid superlatives and self-congratulation.

This. Cinematic landscape and aerial photography. Dubai, UAE.
Not this. Award-winning photographer creating breathtaking landscapes around the world.
— 02

Precise,
not cold.

Use specific, evocative language. Technical accuracy is a form of respect. Name the place, the paper, the hour.

This. Archival pigment on 310gsm Hahnemühle cotton rag.
Not this. High-quality print on premium paper.
— 03

Minimal,
not empty.

Fewer words, more weight. Captions are tight. Descriptions are vivid but brief. White space in writing mirrors white space in design.

This. Wahiba, first light.
Not this. Truly stunning desert vista at the magical golden hour I was lucky enough to witness.
— 04

Personal,
not casual.

First person is fine — this is a personal brand. But the register stays considered, not chatty. Think interview, not group chat.

This. I came back to this dune for three mornings before the light held.
Not this. OMG you guys, this dune is INSANE — had to share!!!

— Operating rules

Practical defaults.

Field-applicable rules for daily writing. When a draft drifts, return to these.

  1. 01 Write in present tense by default. "The light holds" reads stronger than "the light held."
  2. 02 Keep social captions under twenty words. Let the image carry the post.
  3. 03 Lead with material and process for prints. Buyers want to know what they are getting.
  4. 04 One exclamation mark per paragraph at most. Most paragraphs need none.
  5. 05 Hashtags, when used, are minimal (three to five), lowercase, no punctuation.
  6. 06 Title pieces with proper nouns or single concepts. "Atlas" works. "Beautiful Sunset" doesn't.
  7. 07 Edition labels include edition number, technique, paper, grammage, dimensions — in that order.
  8. 08 If a sentence can lose a word without changing meaning, lose the word. Restraint is what the system pays for.
— Words to retire

These five appear in every photographer's about page. The brand earns distance from them by not reaching for them.

  • stunning
  • breathtaking
  • amazing
  • gorgeous
  • unique

Reach instead for the specific noun, the proper place, the technical fact. Wahiba is more vivid than stunning desert. 310gsm cotton rag is more vivid than premium paper.

— A closing note

If a paragraph can be a sentence, make it a sentence.

The brand's voice is not loud. It does not need to be. The work is the loud part.
