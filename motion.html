— Motion

What moves,
moves on the beat.

The brand's other tense. Motion lives in reels, in short films, in the small animations of the website. It carries the same restraint as the still work — but adds a single new instrument: the song. Every cut comes off the music, not off the visual.

— Principle

Song dictates the cut.

The single rule that organises every motion piece in this brand.

Every reel begins with the music. The selection is the first creative decision — tempo, mood, rhythm, the breath between phrases. Once chosen, the song decides everything else: where the cuts land, how long a frame holds, when the piece ends.

The image follows the song. The song carries the image. A frame holds for a phrase, a transition lands on a beat, the piece resolves on a final note. The viewer feels the cut without being told to — the music has already told them.

This is the inversion of standard editing — where music is laid over a cut. Here the music is laid first, and the cut is read off it.

— Cadence

Slow over fast.

Pacing follows the same gravity as the rest of the brand: restraint over urgency, breath over breathlessness, the long held frame over the rapid cut.

— 01

Hold longer than feels safe.

The MB reel corpus averages five seconds per shot — three times slower than the platform norm of one to two. Eight seconds on a single shot is rarely too long; two seconds is rarely enough.

— 02

One transition vocabulary per piece.

Pick a single transition type — straight cut, dip-to-black, match-cut on motion — and use only that. Mixing transitions reads as indecision. Restraint scales.

— 03

No motion for motion's sake.

Every camera move (push, pull, pan, gimbal arc) earns its presence by revealing something. If the same frame would work as a still, leave it as a still.

— 04

Open and close on stillness.

A piece begins with a held frame and ends with one. Movement happens in the middle. The viewer leaves with a still image in the eye, not a fade.

— Specs

Three formats, three uses.

Aspect ratio and length follow the platform. Pick once at the start; do not re-cut for a different format mid-edit — the framing is part of the composition.

Vertical reel 9:16 · 1080 × 1920 · 15–60s
Instagram Reels · TikTok · YouTube Shorts. The default for new motion work.
Landscape film 16:9 · 1920 × 1080 · 60–180s
Website hero · YouTube long-form · gallery wall projection. The format that respects cinematic composition.
Square loop 1:1 · 1080 × 1080 · 6–15s
Instagram feed · email signature loop. Short, looped, designed to feel infinite — like the pattern.

Frame rate · 30fps standard across reels and short films · 60fps for slow-motion drone passes only.

— Sound

Music is voice.

Sound design is part of the brand voice — not a layer added after. The same restraint applies: one instrument earns more attention than seven.

01
Music chosen, not generated.

Every piece uses real music — licensed, attributed, considered. Never library stock that any brand could buy. The song is part of the work.

02
Silence is permitted.

A piece can hold its breath. Diegetic sound — wind, water, footsteps — sometimes carries more weight than music. Pick silence when the image is loud enough on its own.

03
No voice-over.

The brand does not narrate itself. If a piece needs explanation, it has not been edited tightly enough. Let captions do the explaining where they must.

04
Audio attribution.

Track and artist are credited at the end of every piece. A small line in mono type, lower-right, under the brand anchor.

— A working test

Before locking a cut, ask: would this still work without the song?

If yes — it's not yet using the music. The cut should depend on the song so completely that the song removed would expose the seams.

That dependency is the goal, not a flaw.

— Discipline

What the motion leaves out.

The same negative rules as the still work — translated to time.

— 01

Kinetic typography.

No animated text overlays, no scrolling captions, no words flying in. Type appears as title cards or end credits — held still, on a clean ground.

— 02

Speed ramps as filler.

Slow-motion is a deliberate choice for a specific subject. Never used to pad a cut or "make it cinematic" — the cinematic comes from the frame, not the speed.

— 03

Filters and LUT presets.

The footage is colour-graded by hand to match the still photography rules — warm reads warm, cool reads cool. No preset look. No "cinematic" filter.

— A closing note

A reel is a still photograph that took its time.

Motion is the same brand, with one extra instrument. The song decides; the rest follows.
