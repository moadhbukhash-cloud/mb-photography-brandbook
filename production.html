— Production & Packaging

The print
survives the file.

Photography lives in print. Each surface — the tube, the card, the wrap, the seal — is part of how the work arrives. The pattern earns its strongest expression here: full-bleed, on the inside, where the surprise happens.

Materials
Uncoated fine art papers · GF Smith Colorplan
Print process
Single-color offset · letterpress · screen
Finish
Matte uncoated · soft-touch · debossed monogram
— Packaging

What surrounds
the print.

Four touchpoints — the print tube, the thank-you cards, the tissue and wraps, the seals and tags. Each made from the same monochrome system, sized to a print fulfillment that arrives whole.

— Photo tube

The tube, end to end.

Mailing tubes are wrapped end-to-end in the pattern. The wrap is the brand experience before the print is unrolled. Pattern at full density; cap in ink; label in mono.

— Merchandise

What the work
travels with.

Beyond the print and its packaging — the brand worn, carried, drunk from, written in. Each piece follows the same monochrome system. Pattern accents earn their place; the logomark stays restrained.

— Apparel

Worn quietly.

Heavyweight cotton, single-colour print. The chest mark is the logomark; the cap brim carries the pattern as an inside-only reveal.

— Carry

Tote and bandana.

The pattern hides on the inside of the tote and rides openly on the bandana — two opposite postures, same vocabulary.

— Tools & drinkware

Field-ready.

Things that travel with the camera. The pattern lives on the back of the phone case and the lower band of the mug — surfaces that turn into the hand.

— Print goods

Paper objects.

Three weights of print product — the saddle-stitched zine, the postcard pack, the hardcover photo book. Same paper family across all three, scaled to scale.
