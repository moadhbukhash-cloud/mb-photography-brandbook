— Principles

A discipline,
not a decoration.

Three principles guide every decision in this system. They are not aesthetics; they are constraints. When a choice feels difficult, return to them.

— 01

Frame, then fill.

The image is the subject. Every element of the system — type, rule, mark — exists to frame the photograph, never to compete with it.

— 02

Architecture over ornament.

The monogram, the grid, the rules — all geometric. Aerial work reveals pattern in the world; the identity reveals pattern in itself.

— 03

Silence is a tool.

Negative space is not the absence of content; it is content. Generous margins, quiet pages, long pauses between lines.

— The Graphic Device

A monogram, repeated until it becomes terrain.

The MB mark, abstracted into a square tile of cells, becomes the graphic device. Cascaded into a corner, it becomes the brand anchor — the signature we apply to every format we produce.
